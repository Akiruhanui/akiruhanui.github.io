About the Project

Reusing headphones in a helmet.

FIND BETTER IMAGES FOR THIS PAGE

This project was a very simple repurposing project. I had a pair of headphones that I enjoyed using, but after around 3 years of heavy use the plastic frame began to break and one ear cup broke off. I decided to repurpose the headphones so that they could be used in a bike helmet. For this project, I also made sure to use existing cavities in the helemt, as modifying the helmet would have affected the integrity of the helemt and would have made it unsafe to use.

I disassembled the headphones and retrieved the speakers and wire from the plastic enclosure. The plastic connected to the speakers was cut enough so that the upper part did not apply pressure to the side of the user’s head. The lower part was retained so that the ear cushions could still be kept in place. The wires were resoldered to the speakers and were positioned into an empty location of the helmet (no indents were created in the helmet so that its structural integrity remained).

To allow for bluetooth compatibility, a simple bluetooth receiver was bought online and taped to one side, which had a 3.5 mm audio jack into which the headphone’s connector went.
